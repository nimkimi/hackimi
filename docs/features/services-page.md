# Feature: Services Page

## Context

Visitors from small businesses don't know if Nima is available for hire or what exactly he offers. Without a services page, the portfolio reads as a showcase rather than a business. This is the most important conversion page for a freelance portfolio — it answers "what do you do, for whom, and how do I hire you?"

## Scope

**In scope:**
- New `/services` route
- Sections: what's offered (web apps, landing pages, e-commerce, maintenance/support), who it's for (small businesses, local companies, early-stage startups), engagement types (project-based, retainer)
- Clear CTA at the bottom → contact / book a call
- Add "Services" to the main navigation

**Out of scope:**
- Detailed case studies (separate feature)
- Legal/contract terms

## Open Questions

- Include pricing ranges or keep them off the page? (Ranges reduce tyre-kickers; hiding them means more discovery calls)
- Describe tech stack (Next.js, React, etc.) or keep it in plain business language?
- Standalone `/services` page or combine with a `/hire` or `/work-with-me` route?
