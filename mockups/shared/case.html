Be My Guide

Matching visually-impaired Norwegians with sighted volunteer guides for running, hiking, and skiing.

  • Next.js 16
  • TypeScript (strict)
  • Auth.js v5
  • Neon Postgres
  • Prisma
  • Upstash Redis
  • Web Push
  • Vercel BotID
  • Vitest

01

Context

Be My Guide is a personal product addressing a real accessibility gap in Norway. Visually-impaired people who want to run, hike, or ski outdoors need a sighted guide alongside them — but finding one is ad-hoc, relying on word-of-mouth and scattered group chats. The goal: make that match dependable instead of accidental.

02

My role

Solo full-stack engineer. I own the product end to end — discovery and scope, system architecture, data modelling, and implementation — making the engineering trade-offs as well as the product calls.

03

Problem

Connect a guide-seeker's request to nearby, willing volunteers quickly and reliably — with timely notifications — without ever exposing sensitive user data. The hard parts are reliability (a request must reach real people, fast), privacy (location and disability data are sensitive), and abuse resistance (an open volunteer network is a tempting target).

04

Approach

A request-broadcast matching model: a guide-seeker's request fans out to all eligible guides, and the first to accept takes it — favouring speed and real availability over a brittle "best single match" algorithm.

  • — Next.js 16 App Router with strict TypeScript for an end-to-end typed surface.
  • — Auth.js v5 for sessions and role-aware access (seeker, guide, admin).
  • — Neon Postgres + Prisma for the data model, keeping sensitive fields scoped and access-controlled.
  • — Upstash Redis rate-limiting and Vercel BotID for abuse protection on open endpoints.
  • — Web Push so a broadcast reaches guides instantly, even off-page.
  • — Vitest covering the matching and authorization logic.

05

Result

The core experience is in place: onboarding captures role and profile, and an admin review flow vets new guides before they go live. The product is in active development — honestly, not yet shipped to the public — but the central request-broadcast flow is architecturally complete, from authenticated request through eligibility fan-out to push notification and acceptance.
