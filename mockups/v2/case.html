01 / Context

An ad-hoc matching problem

In Norway, visually-impaired people who want to run, hike, or ski often need a sighted guide. Today that matching is ad-hoc — word of mouth, scattered Facebook groups, and personal networks. There's no shared, accessible place to find a guide nearby when you actually want to go out.

02 / My role

Solo full-stack

I'm building this end-to-end on my own — product thinking, accessibility, interface, data model, auth, and infrastructure. The constraint forces honest prioritisation: ship the smallest flow that's genuinely usable by someone relying on a screen reader, then expand.

03 / Problem

Matching has to feel safe and fast

A guide request is time-sensitive and trust-sensitive. The product has to reach the right nearby people quickly, keep the experience fully accessible, and stay resilient against spam and abuse — without adding friction for the people who need it most.

04 / Approach

Request-broadcast matching

Instead of one-to-one pairing, a request fans out to nearby eligible guides; the first to accept connects. The stack is chosen to keep that loop fast, safe, and accessible:

05 / Result

Working onboarding, in active development

Honestly: it's not shipped yet. What works today is the onboarding flow — role selection plus profile capture — and an admin review flow for vetting guides. The matching broadcast and notification loop are the current focus. I'm building it in the open and iterating on the accessibility of each screen as I go.
